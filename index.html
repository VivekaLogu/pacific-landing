Liquidity Pool

Liquidity pools are one of the foundational technologies behind the current DeFi ecosystem

They are an essential part of automated market makers (AMM), borrow-lend protocols, yield farming, synthetic assets, on-chain insurance, blockchain gaming â€“ the list goes on.

The idea is profoundly simple. A liquidity pool is basically funds put together in a big digital pile. Users called liquidity providers (LPs) add an equal value of two tokens in a pool to create a market. In exchange for providing their funds, they earn trading fees from the trades that happen in their pool, proportional to their share of the total liquidity.

When youâ€™re executing a trade on an AMM, you donâ€™t have a traditional counterparty. Instead, youâ€™re executing the trade against the liquidity in the liquidity pool.

For the buyer to buy, there doesnâ€™t need to be a seller, only sufficient liquidity in the pool.

Auto-compounding

Distributing new tokens in the hands of the right people is a very difficult problem for crypto projects. Liquidity mining has been one of the more successful approaches.

In summary, the tokens are distributed algorithmically to users who put their tokens into a liquidity pool. Then, the newly minted tokens are distributed proportionally to each userâ€™s share of the pool.

For example, a popular trading pair on the Binance Smart Chain on the PancakeSwap DEX is BNB/CAKE.

LPs providing liquidity to the pool will get rewards in CAKE as part of PancakeSwapâ€™s token emission rewards, plus a share of the transaction fees generated from BNB/CAKE trade swaps.

Traditionally, users who receive newly minted CAKE rewards need to convert CAKE into equal values of BNB and CAKE to stake LP tokens again in the BNB/CAKE LP to earn more CAKE rewards.

Pacific DeFi makes this process much easier by auto-compounding the CAKE rewards of the BNB/CAKE LP, at the same time distributing its native PACIFIC token to LPs as an additional reward for using our ecosystem.

Staking PACIFIC

Users can stake their tokens to receive rewards in PACIFIC during the minting period of 200 days. Once the minting period is over, users will receive all other fees mentioned via dividends or buybacks.

With the PoS mechanism, new blocks are produced and verified by staking. This means that you donâ€™t need special computers to solve difficult math problems, which was the case with traditional proof-of-work (PoW) mining.

Staking is all about how many coins you are holding. The amount determines your reward. A user who has secured more of a certain coin is more likely to be chosen as the new block validator.

The main attraction to staking is that users can lock up their crypto assets and earn passive income. Secondly, staking gives users the opportunity to participate in the governance model of the Pacific DeFi ecosystem.

Liquidity mining is a term used in DeFi applications where users supply

liquidity to decentralized financial applications and receive rewards for doing so.

In the context of Pacific DeFi, liquidity mining refers to users (Liquidity Providers, or LPs) supplying both assets to a given trading pair market so that the protocol can execute trades.

Whenever liquidity is deposited into a pool, PACIFIC tokens are minted to the Liquidity Providerâ€™s address, in proportion to how much liquidity they contributed to the pool. These tokens are a representation of a Liquidity Providerâ€™s contribution to a pool.

Whenever a trade occurs, a 0.3% fee is levied and is distributed pro-rata to all Liquidity Providers in the pool. The user is able to claim the fees when they take their assets back from the protocol.

As liquidity mining is the first stage of Pacific DeFi, you can join our LP pools by converting your assets into our tokens and depositing them into our pools via PancakeSwap. You will receive rewards that are higher or lower depending on the investment amount you've put in the project.
